The article discusses the importance of aligning executive compensation with company strategy to drive better performance. It explores the challenges and key elements that boards must consider when designing compensation packages, such as the proportion of fixed versus variable pay, short-term versus long-term incentives, cash versus equity, and group versus individual rewards. The article draws on research and experiences of four experts and provides insights on how firms approach executive compensation, including the use of benchmarking and formulaic annual incentive plans. The article also discusses the four dimensions of compensation design and the importance of considering a firm's strategic objectives, ability to attract and retain talent, ownership structure, culture, corporate governance, and cash flow. Finally, the article discusses how the recent pandemic and economic crisis will inevitably change the thinking on compensation and offers recommendations for companies to incorporate metrics that serve stakeholder interests in a broader and more meaningful way.
